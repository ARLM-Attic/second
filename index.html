Hi, and thanks for playing my first game.

You can move this window around at any time.

If you skip the tutorial, you can click Help at any time.

Dunno is a game about rolling dice and making money. Try to make as much money as you can in a limited number of turns.

Your dice are on the left, and you can click them to upgrade, once you have some cash.

On the right are the tabs where you'll control the game.

Click to see the Turn pane. There, click Roll to roll the dice you have.

Rolling your dice gives you stuff!

Gold is for buying dice, upgrading dice, and buying tricks. Most dice award you money when you roll them.

Experience points are for levels. Levels give you bonuses. You keep your XP when you start a new game.

Bonus points are for extra turns. Each extra turn costs more. You get these with Blue dice (explained later).

Tricks are special "moves" you can do, like getting One Pair of the same number in one roll, or Three of a Kind.

Click to see the tricks you have. You start with One Pair.

Tricks usually grant you a bonus on top of what the matching dice give. Click next to learn more.

Click to purchase tricks. Tricks only last for the current game, but they're very important!

Example die rolls:

2, 3: You gain $5

2, 2: You gain $4, plus an extra 1x those earnings ($4) for the One Pair trick.

2, 2, 3, 3: You gain $10, plus $4 for the pair of 2s, plus $6 for the pair of 3s, PLUS $10 for Two Pair, if you've bought that trick.

Click a die on the left to upgrade it for gold.

Sides: Increases the size of your die, giving you a chance for higher rolls. But it makes pairs harder to get.

Plus: Adds a number to your die roll. For instance, a three sides dice with plus 1 (3d+1) can roll between 2 and 4, but never 1.

Multiplier: Multiplies the earnings of the die. Stacks with tricks!

Magic: Lets you choose a color for your die, and increases the power of that color. Each color does something different!

Coloring your die makes it do something extra. More Magic points makes it even better!

White: Normal. Makes money and a little bit of XP.

Red: Earns no money, but slowly upgrades all your other dice.

Green: Always rolls the highest number it can. No bonus from magic points.

Yellow: Earns much more money.

Blue: Earns bonus points instead of money.

Purple: Earns experience points instead of money.

Pink: Increases multiplier with each roll.

Don't be discouraged when you lose!

When you run out of turns, you can start over but keep all the bonuses you got from levelling up. That means the next time through will be easier and faster, especially after the first time.

Click to see the Perks you've gained from levelling up so far.
